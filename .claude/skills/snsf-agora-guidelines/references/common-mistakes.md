# Common Mistakes in SNSF Agora Proposals

## Critical Errors (Likely Rejection)

### 1. Missing Agora Specialist
**Mistake:** Team consists only of researchers without a communication expert.
**Correct:** Include at least one Agora specialist (communication, media, education, or art professional).

### 2. No Dialogue Element
**Mistake:** Project is pure dissemination (videos, lectures, brochures) without interaction.
**Correct:** Include mechanisms for two-way engagement: Q&A sessions, workshops, participatory activities, feedback collection.

### 3. No Link to Research
**Mistake:** Communication project not connected to specific research.
**Correct:** Explicitly link to SNSF-funded research OR peer-reviewed publications.

### 4. Ineligible Project Type
**Mistake:** Marketing, institutional communication, technology transfer, or science communication research.
**Correct:** Focus on communicating scientific knowledge and fostering public dialogue.

### 5. Exceeding Page Limit
**Mistake:** Project plan longer than 5 pages.
**Correct:** Edit ruthlessly to fit within 5 pages.

---

## Structural Errors

### 6. Vague Target Audience
**Mistake:** "The general public" or "Swiss citizens" without specificity.
**Correct:** Define precisely: "Swiss secondary school teachers (ages 25-55) in German-speaking cantons who teach natural sciences."

### 7. Methods Without Justification
**Mistake:** "We will create videos and organize workshops."
**Correct:** Explain WHY these methods work for YOUR specific audience.

### 8. Missing Impact Estimates
**Mistake:** "We hope to reach many people."
**Correct:** Provide specific numbers: "We expect to reach 500 participants through workshops and 10,000 viewers through our video series, based on [comparable project/platform data]."

### 9. No Diversity Statement
**Mistake:** Ignoring the diversity and equal opportunities criterion.
**Correct:** Explicitly address how you'll promote inclusion, reach underrepresented groups, and ensure accessibility.

### 10. Unclear Team Roles
**Mistake:** Listing team members without explaining who does what.
**Correct:** Define specific responsibilities for each team member.

---

## Content Errors

### 11. Academic Jargon
**Mistake:** Using technical language throughout the proposal.
**Correct:** Write accessibly - if you can't explain it simply in the proposal, you can't explain it to the public.

### 12. Overselling Impact
**Mistake:** "This project will transform public understanding of climate science."
**Correct:** Be ambitious but realistic. Cite evidence for impact claims.

### 13. Researcher-Centric Framing
**Mistake:** Focusing on what researchers want to communicate.
**Correct:** Focus on what the AUDIENCE wants/needs to know and how they want to engage.

### 14. Ignoring Risk
**Mistake:** No mention of what could go wrong.
**Correct:** Identify 2-3 key risks with mitigation strategies.

---

## Budget Errors

### 15. Requesting Researcher Salaries
**Mistake:** Including salaries for researchers at Swiss universities.
**Correct:** Researcher salaries are generally not covered. Budget for Agora specialists, external partners, production, and materials.

### 16. Budget-Activity Mismatch
**Mistake:** Budget doesn't align with described activities.
**Correct:** Ensure every major activity has corresponding budget allocation.

### 17. Over/Under Budget
**Mistake:** Budget outside CHF 5,000 - 50,000 range.
**Correct:** Scale project to fit rolling call limits (use standard Agora call for larger projects).

---

## Process Errors

### 18. Wrong Language
**Mistake:** Submitting in German, French, or Italian.
**Correct:** All Agora applications must be in English.

### 19. Starting Before Decision
**Mistake:** Planning to start immediately upon submission.
**Correct:** Allow 4 months for decision. Project start must be after approval.

### 20. Forgetting CV Requirements
**Mistake:** Not including proper CVs for all applicants.
**Correct:** Researchers need SNSF-format CVs. Agora specialists can use narrative CVs with "Major Achievements" section.

---
